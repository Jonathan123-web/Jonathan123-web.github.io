Challenge 3: Supporting arguments

One of the arguments at the inequality talk: there is an increase in education level but falling in-work trainings and very few job opportunities for those who have not studied in university.

From the graph created, we can see that the percentage of teens not receiving higher education has fallen from 16% to around 11% 2010-2019.

Here we have a graph showing the unemployment rate of teens at age 18. The percentage has increased from 16% to 22.5% in just 3 years (2018-2020). This shows that it has become more and more difficult for those who have not studied in university to find a job.
